# Amazon — Hiring Culture & CV Tips

## What Amazon Looks For
Amazon's hiring is structured around **16 Leadership Principles** (LPs). Every interview question, and by extension every CV bullet, is evaluated against them. The most commonly tested LPs are:

1. **Customer Obsession** — start with the customer, work backwards
2. **Ownership** — act on behalf of the company beyond your immediate team
3. **Invent and Simplify** — find simple solutions to complex problems
4. **Dive Deep** — stay connected to details, audit frequently
5. **Deliver Results** — focus on the right inputs and deliver with quality

## CV Tips for Amazon Applications

### Structure Every Bullet Around the STAR Format
Amazon interviewers use STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Your CV bullets should implicitly follow this:
- **Weak**: "Worked on the checkout service"
- **Strong**: "Redesigned checkout service error handling, reducing failed transactions by 23% and recovering $1.2M annual revenue"

### Quantify Everything
Amazon values measurable impact above almost everything else. Every bullet should have a number:
- Users affected, revenue generated/saved, latency improved, error rate reduced
- Percentage improvements, absolute figures, time saved

### Show Ownership
Amazon expects candidates to go beyond their remit. Bullets that show you identified a problem and fixed it unprompted score highly.

### Keywords That Resonate at Amazon
ownership, customer obsession, dive deep, deliver results, invent and simplify,
AWS, S3, Lambda, DynamoDB, EC2, SQS, SNS, Kinesis, CloudWatch,
scalability, reliability, high availability, distributed systems, operational excellence,
cost optimisation, frugality, A/B testing, data-driven, cross-functional

## ATS Notes
Amazon uses Taleo. Keywords from the job description must appear verbatim in your CV.
Include the full names of technologies, not abbreviations only.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Bullets without metrics — Amazon screens heavily on quantified impact
- No evidence of ownership — describing team achievements without personal contribution
- Vague leadership claims not tied to specific outcomes
- CV longer than 2 pages for under 10 years experience
- Missing AWS keywords for technical roles — even familiarity should be mentioned
