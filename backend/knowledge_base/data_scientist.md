# Data Scientist — CV Expectations & Hiring Criteria

## What Recruiters Look For (First 6 Seconds)
- Technical depth: ML frameworks, statistical methods, programming languages
- Applied work: models shipped to production, not just academic experiments
- Business impact tied to models or analysis
- Strong education credentials (relevant degree expected at most companies)

## Must-Have CV Sections
- **Experience** — model development, deployment, and business impact
- **Technical Skills** — languages, frameworks, tools, cloud platforms
- **Education** — BSc/MSc/PhD in quantitative field (maths, stats, CS, physics, engineering)
- **Projects** — Kaggle competitions, research papers, or production ML systems

## Common Weaknesses in Data Scientist CVs
- Describing models built without stating their business impact
- Listing frameworks (scikit-learn, PyTorch) without explaining what was built
- No mention of model deployment or MLOps — companies want production-ready scientists
- Academic-style CVs that emphasise research depth over commercial application
- Missing SQL — nearly every DS role requires it

## Strong Bullet Examples
- "Built churn prediction model using XGBoost achieving 87% AUC, deployed via FastAPI, reducing churn by 14% over 6 months"
- "Developed NLP pipeline using BERT to classify 2M+ customer support tickets, reducing manual tagging effort by 80%"
- "Ran A/B tests across 3 recommendation algorithm variants, leading to 12% uplift in click-through rate"

## Keywords Recruiters and ATS Look For
Languages: Python, R, SQL, Scala
ML Frameworks: scikit-learn, PyTorch, TensorFlow, XGBoost, LightGBM, Keras, Hugging Face
Data Tools: pandas, numpy, Spark, dbt, Airflow, Databricks, Snowflake
Visualisation: Tableau, Power BI, Looker, matplotlib, seaborn
MLOps: MLflow, Kubeflow, SageMaker, model deployment, feature store, A/B testing
Methods: regression, classification, clustering, NLP, computer vision, time series, causal inference

## Seniority Signals
- **Junior DS**: academic projects, internships, Kaggle, foundational ML
- **Mid DS**: owns model lifecycle from exploration to deployment
- **Senior DS**: defines modelling strategy, mentors juniors, cross-functional influence
- **Principal/Staff DS**: technical direction for data science org, works with C-suite

## Red Flags
- No SQL — this is a dealbreaker at most companies
- Only academic research with no production deployment experience
- Listing every framework without depth on any of them
- No mention of how models were evaluated or monitored in production
