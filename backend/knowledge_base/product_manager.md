# Product Manager — CV Expectations & Hiring Criteria

## What Recruiters Look For (First 6 Seconds)
- Evidence of shipping products used at scale
- Cross-functional leadership (engineering, design, data, marketing)
- Business impact: revenue, retention, conversion, activation metrics
- Strategic thinking alongside execution

## Must-Have CV Sections
- **Experience** — product ownership, roadmap decisions, business outcomes
- **Skills** — product tools (Jira, Figma, Mixpanel, Amplitude, SQL)
- **Education** — degree expected; MBA optional but signals ambition at senior level
- **Key Achievements** — a dedicated section works well for senior PMs

## Common Weaknesses in PM CVs
- Describing features shipped without stating the business outcome
- No mention of discovery or research — good PMs validate before building
- Missing quantified metrics: MAU, DAU, NPS, revenue, churn
- Describing team output as personal output (credibility risk if caught)
- Overly technical CVs (ATS risk) or overly vague CVs (recruiter risk)

## Strong Bullet Examples
- "Led 0-to-1 launch of mobile checkout feature that increased conversion rate by 18% and drove $2.3M additional ARR in Q3"
- "Prioritised and shipped 14-item roadmap for core product area, reducing customer churn by 9% over two quarters"
- "Ran discovery interviews with 40 enterprise customers, identifying 3 unmet needs that shaped 6-month roadmap"

## Keywords Recruiters and ATS Look For
Strategy: product strategy, roadmap, OKRs, KPIs, go-to-market, market research
Execution: sprint planning, backlog, user stories, agile, scrum, stakeholder management
Data: A/B testing, analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Looker, SQL, funnel analysis
Design: Figma, UX, user research, wireframes, prototyping
Business: revenue growth, retention, conversion, NPS, churn, ARR, DAU, MAU

## Seniority Signals
- **APM/Junior PM**: feature-level ownership, works within defined roadmap
- **PM**: full product area ownership, cross-functional collaboration
- **Senior PM**: multiple product areas, mentors PMs, strong stakeholder management
- **Director/VP**: org-level strategy, resource allocation, hiring and team building

## Red Flags
- No metrics on any bullet — PMs are expected to be data-driven
- Only describing outputs (features) not outcomes (impact)
- No mention of working with engineering or design
- Generic bullets that could apply to any product at any company
